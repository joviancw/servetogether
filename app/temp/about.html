Why does this matter?
Large scale disasters bring out the best intentions of tech savvy humanitarians. There are now dozens of remote digital response organizations ready and willing to lend their skills. The recent typhoon in the Philippines has activated this network. An unfortunate side effect of the overwhelming response is a crippling lack of coordination between responders on the ground and the digital humanitarian network and between networks working remotely. Information about digital response efforts is siloed, often causing confusion and redundant effort - so a team came together to create a platform enabling open coordination of that digital response.

How does this work?
Serve Together is a simple platform that enables needs and skills to be matched in service of digital humanitarian work.

Three key functions create an interface for global collaboration:

  • Learn More
    You can go here to learn more about the digital humanitarian movement, the response organizations we are engaging with, and how you can make a difference.
  • Volunteer
    You can view needs and offer your skills here.
  • View Projects
    You can view ongoing digital humanitarian efforts here.
